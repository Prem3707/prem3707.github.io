02 About

The best systems
disappear. People
just get more done.

I think about automation in terms of leverage. Not just "what can I build", but what should never need human attention again. Every repetitive process, every decision that follows a pattern, every report someone has been producing manually for years. That's where I want to be.

I'm drawn to the space where technical and business thinking overlap. Building the model is one thing. Understanding why it matters, who it changes, and whether it's solving the right problem in the first place. That's the harder and more interesting work.

I'm early in my career, and I know it. What I bring is a clear sense of direction, a habit of going deeper than required, and a curiosity that doesn't really switch off. I believe the people who shape how AI gets used at work will matter more than the models themselves.
